Precipitable water vapor estimation using GNSS

Jan 1, 2016
Photo by NASA on Unsplash

Precipitable water vapor is a crucial variable for weather forecast and climate studies. The signals of Global Navigational Satellite System (GNSS) satellites are used to extract the amount of precipitable water vapor in near-realtime. My research interest in this area is to derive model for estimating mean atmospheric temperature which is an input for extract the amount of precipitable water vapor from GNSS signals.
